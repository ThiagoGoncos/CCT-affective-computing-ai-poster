# Affective Computing: Understanding the Emotional Side of Artificial Intelligence

## Project Overview

This project was developed as part of the **Artificial Intelligence module** at **CCT College Dublin**.

The assignment consisted of producing an **academic research poster** exploring a modern Artificial Intelligence topic. The selected topic was **Affective Computing**, a field of AI focused on enabling machines to detect, interpret, and respond to human emotions.

The objective of the poster is to explain the concept of affective computing, discuss its real-world applications, and analyse its **ethical and social implications**.

This project is **research-based only** and does not include software development or code implementation.

---

## What Is Affective Computing

Affective Computing is a field of Artificial Intelligence that focuses on developing systems capable of recognising and interpreting human emotions.

It combines knowledge from multiple disciplines, including:

- Artificial Intelligence
- Machine Learning
- Psychology
- Cognitive Science
- Human-Computer Interaction

These systems analyse emotional signals such as facial expressions, voice tone, body language, and physiological data to identify emotional states.

The goal is to create more **empathetic and natural interactions between humans and computers**.

---

## How Affective Computing Works

Affective computing systems typically follow several stages.

### Signal Collection

Emotional data can be collected using different types of sensors and inputs, including:

- Cameras to analyse facial expressions
- Microphones to analyse voice tone
- Wearable sensors to monitor physiological signals such as heart rate or skin temperature

---

### Emotion Recognition

Machine learning models analyse the collected signals to detect emotional states.

These models may classify emotions such as:

- Happiness
- Sadness
- Anger
- Fear
- Surprise

Deep learning algorithms are often used to improve recognition accuracy.

---

### Multimodal Emotion Analysis

Advanced systems combine multiple data sources, such as facial expressions, speech patterns, and text sentiment.

This approach improves the reliability of emotion detection.

---

### System Response

Once emotions are detected, the system can adapt its behaviour.

Examples include:

- Chatbots adjusting their tone when users appear frustrated
- Educational systems adapting content when students appear confused
- Safety systems detecting driver fatigue

---

## Real-World Applications

Affective Computing is applied in several industries.

### Healthcare

Emotion recognition systems can help detect stress, fatigue, or early signs of depression, allowing healthcare providers to intervene earlier.

---

### Education

Learning platforms can adapt the difficulty or pace of educational content depending on student engagement and emotional state.

---

### Customer Service

Emotion-aware chatbots can identify frustration or dissatisfaction and escalate interactions to human agents when needed.

---

### Automotive Safety

Driver monitoring systems use cameras and sensors to detect signs of fatigue or distraction.

If the system detects risk factors such as closed eyes or lack of attention, it can alert the driver or recommend a rest break.

---

## Benefits of Affective Computing

Affective computing can offer several benefits.

### Improved Human–Computer Interaction

Emotion-aware systems create more natural and intuitive interactions between users and technology.

---

### Personalisation

Systems can adapt behaviour based on emotional responses, improving user experience.

---

### Safety Improvements

Emotion detection can help identify risky behaviours, particularly in environments such as driving or healthcare.

---

### Better Decision-Making

Organisations can use emotional insights to improve services, products, and communication.

---

## Ethical and Social Concerns

Despite its potential benefits, affective computing raises several ethical issues.

### Privacy Risks

Emotions represent extremely sensitive personal information.

Users may not always understand how emotional data is collected or used.

---

### Algorithmic Bias

Emotion recognition systems trained on limited datasets may misinterpret emotions from different cultures or demographic groups.

---

### Emotional Manipulation

Emotion-aware technologies could potentially influence human behaviour in areas such as marketing or political campaigns.

---

### Technical Limitations

Emotion detection systems may still face challenges such as poor signal quality or incorrect interpretation of emotional signals.

---

## Ethical Mitigation Strategies

To ensure responsible use of affective computing technologies, several principles should be followed.

### Consent and Transparency

Users must clearly understand what emotional data is being collected and why.

---

### Data Minimisation

Only the minimum amount of data necessary should be collected and stored.

---

### Bias Testing

AI models should be regularly tested using diverse datasets to reduce discrimination.

---

### Human Oversight

Human supervision should remain involved in sensitive decisions involving emotional analysis.
